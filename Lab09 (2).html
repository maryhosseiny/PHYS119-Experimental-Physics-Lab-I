Familiarize Part 2

Variables and Model

A reasonable model for the count-rate of x-rays (or gamma rays) emitted from the source and detected by the detector is $R(x) = R_0 e^{-\mu x}$. where x is the thickness of the paper (number of sheets of paper), is the attenuation coefficient, and R(x) is the count-rate for that thickness of shielding.

  • in python we denote $e^{-\mu x}$ as np.exp(-mu*x)
  • We will adjust two parameters, $R_0$ and , to minimize chi-squared.

Familiarize Part 3

Uncertainty

Radioactive decays are random processes which follow a Poisson distribution, where if we count N events, the uncertainty is $u_N = \sqrt{N}$. This is so that our measurements can be compared, independent of the time for which we count (t), we will convert our measurements from counts (N) to count-rates (R = N/t), $u_R = R\sqrt{\frac{1}{N} + (\frac{u_t}{t})^2}$
